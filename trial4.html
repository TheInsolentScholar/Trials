THE 12 TRIALS OF LUCY

TRIAL 4

Growing Hand Pottery Project

Just as Heracles captured the mighty Erymanthian Boar alive, you must now harness the raw elements of earth and water to create new life through artistic expression.

Your Task: Craft a hollow clay hand, fire it, glaze it, fill with compost, and grow plants from it while documenting the growth process through time-lapse photography which must then be made into a video using Premier Pro and uploaded to YouTube.

This trial combines the ancient art of pottery with the nurturing of living things. The Greeks revered Demeter, goddess of the harvest, and valued the connection between human creation and natural growth.

Like the boar that Heracles had to bring back alive, your plants must thrive under your care. The documentation of this growth through modern technology represents the blending of ancient arts with contemporary methods.

In capturing the Erymanthian Boar, Heracles had to drive it into deep snow to slow it down before capturing it. Similarly, your ceramic creation requires multiple steps and patience: first molding the clay hand, then firing it, glazing it, adding soil, planting seeds, and finally documenting its growth over time. Each stage must be completed in proper sequence, just as Heracles had to carefully plan his approach to the dangerous boar.
