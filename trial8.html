THE 12 TRIALS OF LUCY

TRIAL 8

Astrophotography

As Heracles captured the wild man-eating mares of Diomedes, you must now capture the untamed beauty of the night sky, harnessing the light of distant stars that has traveled for eons to reach your lens.

Your Task: Capture a high-quality wide-field astronomical photograph of the southern night sky from the garden using appropriate astrophotography equipment and techniques.

The ancient Greeks were among the first to map the heavens, naming constellations and tracking celestial movements. Astronomers like Hipparchus created star catalogs that guided sailors and set the foundation for our understanding of the cosmos.

This trial requires patience, technical knowledge, and an appreciation for the vast beauty of our universe. You must understand your equipment, the movement of the Earth, and the ideal conditions for capturing the light of celestial objects.

The mares of Diomedes were wild and dangerous creatures that Heracles had to capture and control. Similarly, astrophotography requires you to "capture" and control light from distant sources - a challenging task that demands precision and timing. Just as Heracles needed to understand the behavior of the mares to succeed, you must understand the movement of celestial bodies across the night sky, the Earth's rotation, and the capabilities of your equipment to create a stunning image of the cosmos.
