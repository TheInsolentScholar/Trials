THE 12 TRIALS OF LUCY

TRIAL 6

Arduino Temperature Sensor

As Heracles used his ingenuity to frighten away the deadly Stymphalian Birds with bronze krotala, you must create a clever system that monitors and responds to changes in its environment.

Your Task: Design and build a functional Arduino-based temperature sensing system for the refrigerator that illuminates LEDs to indicate each degree above zero.

The ancient Greeks were pioneers in science and technology. Archimedes and Hero of Alexandria created remarkable devices that responded to environmental stimuli. You follow in their footsteps by creating a system that observes and reacts to temperature changes.

This trial tests your ability to understand electronic components, write code that interacts with the physical world, and create a practical application that serves a useful purpose.

When Heracles faced the Stymphalian Birds, he needed help from the goddess Athena, who gave him bronze krotala (noise-makers) to startle the birds from their hiding places. Similarly, your Arduino system requires components (sensors and LEDs) working together to reveal what is normally hidden from view - the precise temperature inside a refrigerator. Just as Heracles' solution allowed him to see and then defeat the birds, your creation will make invisible temperature fluctuations visible through light.
